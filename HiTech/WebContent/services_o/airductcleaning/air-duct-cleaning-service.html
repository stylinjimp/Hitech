What Is In Those Air Ducts

All homeowners dust and vacuum regularly, but what about the main air distribution system that circulates the air thoughout the entire home? When was the last time someone cleaned those air ducts?

Dirty air ducts are a major cause of indoor air pollution.  In fact, an air duct system is a breading ground for bacteria, animal dander, mold, and dust mites.  These impurities can cause allergic reactions, breathing difficulties, infections, and disease.

Our machine uses HEPA filters (hospital level) to ensure that dust and other materials removed from the air ducts are not being released back into your home. 

 

Plus, with our video inspection system we are able to see inside the air ducts so we know no spots were missed.

Money Savings
♦ Leaking ducts can decrease the overall efficiency of the heating and cooling system by 10%-30%.

♦ Sealing ducts increase efficiency and lowers energy bills, and the savings will usually pay the cost of repairs 1-2 years.

Comfort
♦ Leaking ducts contribute to rooms that are too hot or cold
  • The HVAC system is designed to deliver a certain amount of air to each room and leaking a portion of that designed air into the attic or basement will diminish the amount of air delivered to the intended space.

Safety
♦ Ductwork leakage can cause "backdrafting" where carbon monoxide from gas appliances are drawn back into the living space, rather than being expelled outdoors.

Improved Indoor Air Quality
♦ Leaking ductwork can cause pressurization issues in the home, which can lead to infiltration of dirt, dust and other pollutants.

Environmental Protection
♦ Sealing ductwork allows us to use less energy by being more efficient with the energy we are using.
